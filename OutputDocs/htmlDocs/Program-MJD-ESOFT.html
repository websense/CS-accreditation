Justification of Program

The Bachelor of Engineering (Honours) is a specialist 4-year degree designed to produce graduates who demonstrate the knowledge, technical capability and practical skills relevant to the contemporary world of engineering. The program is built around the Engineers Australia Stage 1 Competencies for Professional Engineers and integrates course content with work integrated learning, co-curricular activities, and professional skills development. The specialisation in Software Engineering provides students with a deep understanding of the software development process, coding and design skills and project management capabilities required in software engineering. The 17core ICT units in the specialisation include the core units of the Computer Science major including algorithms, data structures, databases computer networks and web development. At level 4 and 5 the program builds on this foundation with a focus on practical aspects of software engineering, including requirements elicitation, software quality and testing, and project management. The Software Engineering major at UWA has been designed to provide you with a solid foundation in software requirements, design, implementation, testing, and professional engineering standards. It also includes advanced topics in cloud and high performance computing and a 1-year (2 unit) capstone design project.

Graduates of the Bachelor of Engineering [Honours] (Software Engineering) will have completed : 17 ICT units, 5 ICT-related engineering units, 4 supporting maths, physics and statistics units, 4 Engineering practice units (0 credit), and a 2-unit ICT research project.

Software Engineering students learn about professional practice throughout their degree. Learning activities include guest lectures from industry, research essays, reflective practice, and extra-curricular events such as club-run events such as Industry Insights. Students complete a two-semester team-based design project (CITS5551/52) conducted in conjunction with a real industry client, together with industry-led workshops on professional skills, providing a structured learning experience to prepare graduates for professional practice. Engineering students complete a Professional Engineering Portfolio, GENG5010, including at least 450 hours of discipline-based work experience, and demonstrating their attainment of the 16 mandatory Elements of Competency outlined in the Engineers Australia Stage 1 Competency Standard for Professional Engineers.
